Data protection statement

The website is provided for the general purpose of assisting the Labour Party and its members in organising events and strategies for securing the election of candidates at elections and promoting its political and philosophical values.

The Data Controller is the individual in having overall responsibility for the website at any time or, where that is more than one individual, an unincorporated association existing for the sole purpose of maintaining the website.

Information here shall not be transferred to any third party except in the specific circumstances described below, or where mandated by an order of a court in the United Kingdom of Great Britain or Northern Ireland, or a statutory requirement.

Storage

Data entered into this website will be stored in an electronic database. This will include answers entered into forms on the website, as well as further information calculated from those answers.

Information may be provided by members about volunteers and this will be stored in the same way. It may include (but is not limited to) name, contact information, address (full or partial) details of events attended and activities undertaken. This information may be collected formally or informally by any means.

Members may also collect further information, via the website or on paper (which will then be entered onto the website by members at a later date), about skills, interests, demographics or anything else but this will only be collected where the individual the information is about has freely volunteered the information and is made aware that it will be used on this website.

Usage

EMERGENCIES. In the event of an emergency in which there is a danger to life, property, or is otherwise likely to involve the attendance of emergency services, members or members of the Party may use any of the information on the website to facilitate dealing with that emergency, including contacting relatives. This is in the vital interests of anyone who’s life or property is at risk. This use of website data is unlikely to cause harm and it is not an unreasonable or unexpected use of the data.

PLANNING AND CONVENING LABOUR PARTY EVENTS. Data on this website will be used for the purposes of planning, organising, and carrying out events or other activities pursuant to the political aims of the Labour Party. It is in the legitimate interest of the website, its members, and members of the Party, to promote the political values of the Party and secure the election of Party candidates to public office. As the primary purpose of the website and the Party, this use of data should be expected and has a low risk of causing harm to individuals about whom information is recorded. Even in situations where volunteers are unaware of the existence of the website or any specific Party computer system, it should not be unreasonable for them to expect that their contact information will be used (if provided) to plan, convene, and notify them of further such events. Members of the Party will certainly know about and be used to their data being used in such a way.

TRACKING EVENT ATTENDANCE AND STATISTICS. Information will be entered into the website by members to record the attendance of members and volunteers at events, the duration of events, and what activities were carried out at the event. This information can be used in calculations to derive new data about what activities individuals have undertaken and the amount of time spent. It may also be used in statistics about how successful member’s events and strategies are. This information will be passed to members of the Party and may be used by them in the course of their aims and objectives. This is further to the website’s legitimate interest stated above.

DECISIONS REGARDING SUITABILITY TO BE A CANDIDATE FOR THE PARTY. Information held on the website about an individual (“A”) may be passed to members of the Party for the purposes of deciding A’s suitability to be nominated by the Labour Party as a candidate at an election. In particular, this information will include (a) the number of events the member has attended; (b) the duration of time they have spent at events; and (b) the types of activities they have taken part in. Adverse inferences must not be made from any lack of data. It is in the public interest–and the interests of the Party–for the Party, and its members and supporters, to ensure candidates put up for election on its behalf are competent, fully understand and are prepared to undertake the role, and hold values congruent with those of the Party. Although this use of the data may result in an adverse decision being made against them, there is no right for any person to be a candidate (even if A is the incumbent seeking re-election), and there is unlikely to be material reputational damage or significant psychological harm caused.

ASSISTING ELECTION AGENTS AND CANDIDATES. Contact information from the website may be passed by members to any person validly appointed as Election Agent for a candidate nominated by the Labour Party (or where a candidate is acting as their own Election Agent, the candidate). This is for the purpose of contacting supporters of the Party in order to recruit petitioners to support a candidate’s nomination. This is in furtherance of the legitimate interest of members and volunteers of seeking to secure the election of Party candidates to public office.

Contact by email or telephone

The website or any member may contact any member or volunteer using any email address or phone number held. Contact will only be made regarding the usage and purposes detailed above and may be automated or sent by a member using their own personal communication methods with information taken from the website.

AUTOMATED NOTIFICATION OF EVENTS. Members and volunteers will only receive automatically generated messages about events or campaigns if they have specifically requested that they receive such messages.

OFFLINE COMMUNICATION BY MEMBERS. Members may use the information provided about other members and volunteers and contact them using it. This can only be in the pursuit of the purposes described in the usage section and must not be unexpected or unreasonable.

TRANSACTIONAL MESSAGES. The website or those in charge of it may send messages to members or volunteers for the purposes of (a) ascertaining the accuracy of any information held on the website relating to the individual; (b) ascertaining the validity or correctness of their consent or any consent(s) given to another member or third party which relates to the website; (c) administering the registration process or the member’s account; and (d) informing the member that information has been provided about them by a member, directing them to this notice, and informing them of how to have this information corrected or removed. This is all in the interest of the website ensuring it is lawfully holding data according to the data protection principles.

Definitions

"member"
any individual who has completed at least part of the registration process for the website, including the section asking their communication preferences.
"volunteer"
anyone who’s data has been added to the website, either by them having started–but not completing–the registration process or, by someone else adding the information, and who is not listed as an emergency contact.
"website"
the website normally accessible at “www.committeeroom.co.uk”, its associated software, databases, and processes, and the individual or other persons in charge of it.
the "Party"
the Labour Party, and any accounting unit or other unit of the Party.
"members of the Party"
"Party members"
any individual who is a valid member of the Labour Party according to the Party’s rules and procedures. For the purposes of this statement, it will also include anyone else who is not a valid member of the Labour Party but who is undertaking activities which are pursuant to the aims of the Party and who has not expressed support for another political party, or an organisation with objects or stated political or philosophical values which are incompatible with those of the Labour Party
